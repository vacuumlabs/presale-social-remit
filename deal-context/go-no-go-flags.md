# Go/no-go flags — SocialRemit

**Note — V2 gates are soft checkpoints, not hard stops.** These flags surface signals for an informed team conversation at G1. They are not mechanical blockers.

---

## Green — conditions that support proceeding

- **VL has directly relevant prior work.** Boris Vida led the December 2025 SocialRemit architecture — React Native + NestJS BFF + white-label integration. The domain, stack, and client are already known. No ramp-up needed.

- **Named technical stakeholder.** Paul Duncan (Technical Director) is confirmed and back from Singapore (returned ~25 May 2026). He must approve commercial terms before board sign-off. The engagement has a real technical counterpart, not just a business contact.

- **Deal tier T2 is appropriate.** A mobile app + BFF + third-party integrations fits T2 comfortably. Not under- or over-sized for the process.

- **Regulatory and operational readiness confirmed and strengthened.** EMI registration (FCA) complete, $900M liquidity committed, Sumsub contracted, Figma prototype complete. Corridors now confirmed: **Ghana and Nigeria** (signed liquidity arrangements in place). Card gateway (Trust Payments) and open banking provider (Volume) both confirmed as already integrated in Fincode. This is not vaporware — the client has done their groundwork.

- **Warm relationship.** Joseph reached out directly to Boris (bypassing sales), indicating trust in VL's technical credibility. This is a relationship to build on, not a cold RFP.

- **VL delivery team assembled.** Jaro Novák (PM), Rudolf Vido (PO), Andy Birch (CRO, commercial lead) are all engaged. Marcus Davey is in the channel and being consulted on the commercial qualification. Internal alignment meeting planned for 2026-06-01.

## Amber — conditions that need attention before or during Discovery

- **Deferred payment model awaiting VL leadership decision.** Joseph's ask (minimum upfront, rest on go-live, plus equity/options) is non-standard. Andy Birch (CRO) is responsible for the commercial qualification. Boris asked Marcus Davey for an update on 2026-05-29 — no answer yet. Presale effort should not be overcommitted until the CRO's answer is known.

- **No hard budget figure.** Joseph has not named a budget figure. The December 2025 T&M estimate (£118–169k) is superseded and must not be used as a baseline — it was scoped for RemitONE. The freelancer reference quotes (£5k–£10k) suggest a possible large expectation gap. This must be surfaced before commercial terms are finalised.

- **Timeline is aggressive, though prior work reduces risk.** 6–8 weeks for a full MVP (KYC, multi-currency wallet, two payment rails, card + open banking) is tight. Joseph's 28 May response explains the rationale: Figma UX is substantially done, providers are already in Fincode's ecosystem, corridors are ready. These reduce build risk, but the PM must still pressure-test the timeline in Discovery and the proposal must set realistic expectations. Open to phasing discussion per Joseph.

- **Flutter vs React Native is unresolved.** VL leans React Native (more experience, larger hiring pool), but the recommendation needs mobile expert input. If Flutter is ultimately chosen, resourcing and timeline assumptions in the proposal may shift.
