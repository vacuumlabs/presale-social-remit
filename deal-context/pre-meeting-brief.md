# Pre-meeting brief — SocialRemit

_Read this immediately before the next client interaction. Target: under 3 minutes._

**Context note:** The May 19 discovery call with Joseph has already taken place. This brief is framed for the **proposal delivery meeting** (~w/c 25 May 2026, when Paul Duncan returns from Singapore).

---

## 1. The client and the ask

SocialRemit is a UK fintech startup — EMI registered, FCA regulated — building a consumer remittance mobile app for UK-based migrants. They want VL to deliver a Flutter or React Native app plus a BFF middleware layer connected to Fincode's white-label backend, live within 6–8 weeks.

---

## 2. What we know

- **This is a re-engagement.** VL proposed £118–169k T&M in December 2025 (Boris led architecture). That proposal was never contracted. SocialRemit is now ready — EMI registered, Fincode selected, Sumsub contracted, Figma prototype complete.
- **Joseph controls the relationship; Paul controls technical sign-off.** Joseph (FCCA, finance background) reached out directly to Boris in May 2026, bypassing sales. Paul Duncan (Technical Director) must approve commercial terms before board sign-off. Paul is back from Singapore on 25 May.
- **The commercial model is the single biggest risk.** Joseph has asked for a deferred payment structure (minimum upfront, rest on go-live) plus equity/options. This is non-standard for VL and is under CEO/CDO review. The proposal cannot be finalised until VL leadership answers.

---

## 3. What we need to find out

1. **What is VL's position on the deferred payment model?** (Internal — Andy Birch (CRO) is responsible for the commercial qualification. Boris asked Marcus Davey for an update on 2026-05-29; answer pending. This is the single remaining gate before the proposal can be finalised.)
2. **Flutter or React Native?** VL leans React Native — Boris must confirm with VL mobile experts and include the recommendation in the proposal.

_Resolved since 19 May:_ MVP corridors (Ghana + Nigeria confirmed), Fincode API docs (full gap analysis produced), admin/back-office tool (Fincode + Sumsub dashboards cover go-live needs; lightweight tooling TBC after Fincode walkthrough).

---

## 4. What we bring

VL has directly relevant experience: Boris led the December 2025 SocialRemit architecture (React Native + NestJS BFF + white-label integration), which provides a proven baseline for this re-engagement. VL's differentiators for this client are payments domain expertise, pre-built auth and progressive KYC patterns (Sumsub), and the BFF abstraction approach that protects SocialRemit's 12-month Fincode exit plan. The longer-term vision (Affecta Technology group — Transpara B2B, E-money Score, E-money Finance) makes this a strategically valuable partnership if SocialRemit reaches traction.

---

## 5. Go/no-go status

**One red flag, six amber signals.** The red flag is the deferred payment + equity commercial model — non-standard for VL, currently under CEO/CDO review. Until that is resolved, do not commit to a fixed proposal format. The six amber signals include an extremely aggressive 6–8 week go-live timeline, no hard budget figure, and the Fincode API remaining unreviewed. None of the ambers are blockers individually, but they compound.

---

## 6. Suggested call agenda

1. **Reconnect and set context** — brief rapport check-in; confirm Paul's involvement and what he needs to see from the proposal.
2. **Scope confirmation** — walk through the MVP in/out-of-scope list; confirm corridors, must-haves vs nice-to-haves; invite Joseph and Paul to adjust.
3. **Commercial terms discussion** — present VL's position on the payment model (informed by Tomas / Marek's answer); explain the rationale clearly; agree a structure or narrow the gap.
4. **Timeline and team** — present a realistic delivery roadmap; name the go-live risks; agree on a phasing approach if 6–8 weeks requires scope reduction.
5. **Next steps** — NDA confirmation, Fincode sandbox access, Figma link, and proposed contract kickoff date.
