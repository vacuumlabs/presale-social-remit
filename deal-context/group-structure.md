# SocialRemit Group Structure — Affecta Technology

Disclosed by Joseph Owusu on the 19 May 2026 call. The board has decided all IP sits in **Affecta Technology**, the group IP holding company. Individual products are subsidiaries or branded products within the group.

## Group entities

| Entity | Description | Stage |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| **Affecta Technology** | IP holding company — owns all group IP | Active |
| **E-money Financial Services / Social Remit** | Trading entity. Cross-border remittance app. "Built by migrants, for migrants." Primary near-term focus. | MVP build in progress |
| **E-money Pay** | Branded unique features for Social Remit | Active (product layer) |
| **Transpara** | B2B product: end-to-end remittance and wallet infrastructure leased to other small remittance businesses. Solves reconciliation, treasury, fraud, and operational issues. SocialRemit's own future white-label offering. | Future |
| **E-money Score** | Credit and behavioural scoring engine. Joseph started this concept in 2016 (discussed with Cabinet Office / Parliament). Now being built as part of the ecosystem. | Future |
| **E-money ESG** | Impact and trust layer | Future |
| **E-money Finance** | Credits, savings, embedded finance, EWA (Earned Wage Access) | Future |

## Strategic intent

Joseph's stated valuation ambition ("five, seven, ten years") is not achievable through cross-border payments alone. The group is designed so that each product reinforces the others: remittance provides the customer base and behavioural data, e-money Score converts that data into credit products, Transpara monetises the infrastructure B2B, and E-money Finance captures savings and embedded finance. Transpara in particular positions SocialRemit as a future infrastructure provider to the market they're currently a participant in.

## Relevance to VL engagement

The immediate ask is Social Remit MVP (E-money Pay). But the long-term relationship — if the MVP goes well — likely spans Transpara (building the proprietary backend that replaces Fincode) and potentially E-money Score. Joseph explicitly framed this as "not just a one-off project."
