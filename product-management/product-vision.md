# Product Vision & Differentiation

Articulated by Joseph Owusu on the 19 May 2026 call.

## Brand

- **Name:** Social Remit (went through a rebranding period, now back to Social Remit)
- **Trading as:** E-money Financial Services
- **E-money = Emotional Money**
- **Tagline:** "Money is emotional — make your money work the way life actually works"
- **Sub-tagline:** "Built by migrants, for migrants"
- **Brand colour:** Coral
- **Logo concept:** Two mountains — the foreground (darker, smaller) represents the struggles migrants face abroad; the background (taller, faint) represents the unseen struggle of keeping home — sending money to pay bills and family back in the origin country. "Nobody sees it except the migrants."

## Differentiation

Joseph's positioning map of the competitive landscape:

| Brand | Identity |
|-------|----------|
| Revolut | "Do more for your money" — super app |
| Monzo | "Feel better about your money" — reassurance |
| Wise | Transparency, cost-efficiency |
| TapTap / Western Union / WorldRemit | Speed — send money fast from A to B |
| **Social Remit** | "Let your money work the way life actually works" — emotional intelligence |

The core claim: every competitor was designed by people outside the migrant experience. Social Remit is the first designed by people living it. Joseph was CEO/CFO/treasury at UnityLink, where he scaled the business from 50,000 to 1–1.5 million transactions per day manually. The innovations he introduced there (e.g., speaking to customers in their local language — now industry standard) form part of Social Remit's 6-year innovation pipeline.

## Innovation roadmap

Joseph describes a ~6-year pipeline of product innovations targeted at migrants and low-income workers — things competitors have not built because they were designed from the outside. He expects competitors to copy each feature within ~6 months of launch, which is why the pipeline depth matters. Specific features not disclosed in available sources.

## MVP product experience (from Figma prototype walk-through)

- Welcome screen → language selection → OTP registration (phone number only, no details collected upfront)
- PIN creation on first login; biometric option
- **Gateway screen** (unusual): instead of a dashboard, the customer is asked "What do you want to do today?" — send money, check rates, etc. Deliberate departure from traditional dashboard-first apps.
- Traditional greeting ("How do you want us to greet you?") — localised, personalised
- Send money flow: select corridor → amount → KYC routing (amount-based, automatic) → beneficiary → payment method (card / open banking / wallet) → confirmation → transfer sent
- Wallet top-up via open banking or card (top-up button on home screen — noted as needing to be more prominent)
- Transaction history, referral section, profile/settings

## Target user

UK-based migrants and low-income workers sending money internationally. Specific corridors: two countries confirmed (names not disclosed in available sources). Joseph confirmed this is a consumer-only product for MVP.
